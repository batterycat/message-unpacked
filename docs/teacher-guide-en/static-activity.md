# Static activity links

Static activities are the simplest teaching path. The chosen case IDs,
versions, topic, duration, and facilitation mode are encoded in a shareable URL.
The URL contains no student identity or answer history.

## Student self-paced

Share the link or QR Code. Each learner chooses an answer, reviews the score and
reasoning, and sees a final learning summary. Answers and progress remain in the
current browser page and are not sent to a project-operated database.

## Teacher projector-led

Project one activity for the whole class. Ask learners to identify the request,
evidence, and safer next action before choosing the class answer. The teacher
then reveals the explanation, score, source information, and official help
resources.

This mode does not collect individual answers or create live statistics. Use a
show of hands, paper notes, or verbal voting when a simple class response is
useful.

## Suggested routine

1. Read the complete message.
2. Identify what the sender asks the recipient to do.
3. Separate evidence from claims made by the message itself.
4. Choose a lower-risk action even when the evidence is incomplete.
5. Review the explanation and discuss how to verify through a trusted channel.
