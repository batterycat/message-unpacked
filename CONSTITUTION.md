# Message, Unpacked. Project Constitution

This constitution defines the non-negotiable rules for every release and
contribution. Convenience, visual polish, and publication deadlines never
override these rules.

## 1. Factual Integrity

- Clearly label each case as documented, composite, classic pattern, or
  fictional. Never imply that a composite or teaching fiction is a single real
  event.
- Every factual claim about a documented incident must point to a reviewable
  source. Every number must include its scope or qualifier and source.
- Missing information is unknown, not zero. Disputed or unavailable evidence is
  removed, qualified, or causes the case to be unpublished.
- Sources are cited with publisher, canonical URL, relevant date, access date,
  and usage review. Citation does not grant copyright permission.

## 2. Licensing and Attribution

- Project code is Apache-2.0. Original educational content is CC BY-SA 4.0.
  Third-party material remains under its own terms.
- Before adding code, dependencies, Actions, fonts, icons, images, audio,
  screenshots, datasets, quotations, brand imitations, or adapted content,
  record and review it in `docs/THIRD_PARTY_RESOURCES.md`.
- Unknown, custom, ambiguous, or incompatible terms block inclusion. “Found
  online,” educational intent, and attribution alone are not permission.
- Preserve all required copyright, LICENSE, NOTICE, source-offer, and
  attribution material. Modified files retain any file-level copyleft duties.

## 3. Privacy and Student Safety

- Do not collect student names, contact details, real messages, identifiers, or
  behavioural analytics in the static MVP.
- Publish a plain-language scope and privacy notice for every hosted service.
  New data flows, analytics, classroom synchronization, accounts, or message
  analysis require a new privacy review before release.
- Remove personal information from source events and use reserved domains,
  numbers, and fictional identities in exercises.
- Never create clickable live scam links or actions that contact suspicious
  parties. Official calls and external sites require clear user confirmation.
- Do not use humiliation, victim-blaming, deceptive consent, forced urgency, or
  emotionally manipulative surprise as a teaching device.
- Sensitive situations must carry content warnings and trusted-adult guidance
  appropriate to the learning stage.

## 4. Educational Quality

- Reward safe reasoning and verification actions, not only a correct fraud
  label. Include trustworthy and insufficient-evidence cases to prevent
  reflexive “everything is fraud” behaviour.
- Explain persuasion techniques, evidence, consequences, and practical next
  steps. Avoid presenting technical confidence as a substitute for verification.
- Published cases use the reviewed locale registry's fixed online-verification
  and consultation resource IDs. They appear after debrief as optional help,
  never as proof that every message is fraud or must be reported.
- Official names, destinations, owners, and verification dates live in the
  locale resource registry rather than individual case files.

## 5. Accessibility and Inclusion

- Core experiences must work with keyboard navigation and screen-reader reading
  order and must not rely on colour, sound, timing, or pointer precision alone.
- Use plain, age-appropriate language and respectful descriptions of affected
  people. Avoid stereotypes about age, education, disability, or technical
  ability.
- Automated accessibility checks are necessary but do not replace manual review.

## 6. Internationalization

- The interface supports `zh-TW` and `en`; learner content declares its own
  locale independently.
- Missing interface translations fail validation. Missing case translations are
  reported honestly and must never be filled by silently relabelling Chinese
  content as English.
- A translation is a reviewed educational work with its own content version and
  review date, not an untracked copy.

## 7. Review and Enforcement

- Structured content and dependencies must pass automated validation and human
  review before publication.
- A contributor may stop publication when evidence, safety, privacy,
  accessibility, or licensing is uncertain. The burden is on inclusion to prove
  compliance.
- Exceptions require a documented amendment to this constitution; they cannot
  be hidden in a case file, pull request comment, or build configuration.
